Yabatech

Yaba College of Technology is the cradle of Higher Education in Nigeria. Established in 1947 as an immediate successor to Yaba Higher College,Yaba College of Technology, YABATECH as it is fondly referred to attained autonomous and independent status in 1969 by virtue of Decree 23 which granted it the exclusive mandate to provide full-time and part-time courses of instructionand training in Technology, Applied Science, Commerce and Management and in such other fields of applied learning relevant to technical, vocational and industrial needs of Nigeria. The College is well acknowledged both within and outside Nigeria as a pace setter and a unique Centre of Learning and Teaching. It has forged ahead and repositioned itself as an institution of class and excellence. The vision of the college, is "to be the leading higher educational institution in Nigeria by providing first-rate academic, professional and entrepreneurial education to our students, who are empowered to make a positive impact in the technological and socio-economic development of the country" and the mission therefore is "to produce knowledgeable and innovative graduates, worthy in skill and character, through effective teaching, learning and research for the technological advancement of Nigeria".

ABOUT MSSN YABATECH

Mssn Yabatech is a branch of Mssn Yaba Area Council which represents Mssn Lagos State Area Unit being the largest student body. Our goal is to instill spiritual consciousness together with academic excellence amongst Muslim students. Our administrative focus is Leadership, Productivity and Steadfastness. We can boast of 32 executives both brothers and sisters to ensure our aims and objectives are achieved. We are also supported by school co-ordinators and co-ordinatress at the departmental level. There are 8 schools at present in yabatech with each school having her own Muslim association. They are organ's of Mssn Yabatech with the same focus. They help in fulfilling some of the objectives. Not only this, there are lot of programmes which have been put in place to help achieve our objectives. Starting from the orientation week for the freshers, weekly scholars in the house and monthly open air lectures, fajr bayan, madrasah and tahfeedh classes, Fiqh classes, Dawah camp which comes up after first semester examination, the jihad week named I.P.W (Islamic Propagation Week) and it ends with the annual conference which is aimed at nominating the new executives for the next adminstration. We're not left alone in this cause, but rather supported by our Fathers at the Yabatech Muslim Community, which is chaired by our former Deputy Rector, Dr Ma'ruf Adebakin and the Chief Imam Raji Abdulahafeez. They give the needed support to ensure the proper execution of dawah and proper conduct of Muslim Students on campus.
